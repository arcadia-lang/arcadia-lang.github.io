# <x-trans>Cases</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia has **seven grammatical cases**, each marked by a **suffix**.</x-trans>
<x-trans>These cases define the **syntactic role** of a noun within a sentence.</x-trans>

<x-trans>We will use **"pan"** (_bread_) as our example noun.</x-trans>

## **<x-trans>Case System</x-trans>**

- **<x-trans>Nominative</x-trans>** (**"-is"**) → <x-trans>Marks the **subject** of the sentence</x-trans>: _panis_
- **<x-trans>Accusative</x-trans>** (**"-on"**) → <x-trans>Marks the **direct object**</x-trans>: _panon_
- **<x-trans>Dative</x-trans>** (**"-im"**) → <x-trans>Marks the **indirect object** (recipient)</x-trans>: _panim_
- **<x-trans>Ablative</x-trans>** (**"-a"**) → <x-trans>Marks the **indirect object** (donor/source)</x-trans>: _pana_
- **<x-trans>Predicative</x-trans>** (**"-i"**) → <x-trans>Used with **linking verbs** to express **attributes**</x-trans>: _pani_
- **<x-trans>Adpositional</x-trans>** (**"-e"**) → <x-trans>Used after **prepositions**</x-trans>: _pane_
- **<x-trans>Vocative</x-trans>** (**"-o"**) → <x-trans>Used for **direct address**</x-trans>: _pano_

## **<x-trans>Vowel Merging Rules</x-trans>**

<x-trans>If a noun **ends in a back vowel** (_&{a}, &{o}, &{u}_), the suffix merges with the vowel:</x-trans>

- [x] **<x-trans>Nominative</x-trans> & <x-trans>Accusative</x-trans>:** <x-trans>The **suffix vowel is dropped** (_instead of "luna-is" → "lunas"_).</x-trans>
- [x] **<x-trans>Other cases</x-trans>:** <x-trans>The **noun's final vowel is dropped** (_instead of "luna-on" → "lunon"_).</x-trans>
