# <x-trans>Number</x-trans>

<x-trans>Numbers are formed by adding a **suffix** to the root word.</x-trans>
<x-trans>The semantic meaning of the suffix **varies** depending on whether the noun is **countable or uncountable**.</x-trans>

- [x] <x-trans>If the noun **ends in a consonant**, an **"e" is inserted** between the base and the suffix.</x-trans>

---

## **<x-trans>Countable Nouns</x-trans>**

<x-trans>Countable nouns are those that can be **quantified as discrete units**—such as apples, kilograms, and humans.</x-trans>
<x-trans>Using **"dia"** (_day_), here are the suffixes:</x-trans>

- **<x-trans>Singular</x-trans>:** <x-trans>No suffix</x-trans>—<x-trans>used for exactly one item (**dia** → a day).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Fractional</x-trans> (`-d`)** → <x-trans>Represents a quantity **less than one** (**diad** → part of a day).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Plural</x-trans> (`-s`)** → <x-trans>Used for **more than one item**, even if not a full integer (**dias** → days).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Nullar</x-trans> (`-n`)** → <x-trans>Expresses **absence** of the noun (**dian** → no days).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Collective</x-trans> (`-r`)** → <x-trans>Represents the **totality** of the noun (**diar** → all days).</x-trans>

✔ <x-trans>**Negative quantities** take the **same suffix** as their positive counterpart.</x-trans>

---

## **<x-trans>Uncountable Nouns</x-trans>**

<x-trans>Uncountable nouns do **not** have discrete units—such as water, money, and love.</x-trans>
<x-trans>Using **"amore"** (_love_), the suffixes and their meanings are:</x-trans>

- **<x-trans>Singular</x-trans>:** <x-trans>No suffix</x-trans>—<x-trans>used when quantity is unspecified (**amore** → love).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Fractional</x-trans> (`-d`)** → <x-trans>Represents a **small** quantity (**amored** → a little bit of love).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Plural</x-trans> (`-s`)** → <x-trans>Expresses a **large** quantity (**amores** → an abundance of love).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Nullar</x-trans> (`-n`)** → <x-trans>Indicates **absence** (**amoren** → no love).</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Collective</x-trans> (`-r`)** → <x-trans>Represents the **totality of the concept** (**amorer** → all love in existence).</x-trans>
