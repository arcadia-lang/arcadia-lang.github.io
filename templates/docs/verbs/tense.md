# Tense

Arcadia has five tenses, two timeless tenses, and three timed tenses.
These tenses are used to indicate the time frame of an action or state described by a verb.
The two timeless tenses are used for actions or states that are not bound by time, while the three timed tenses indicate actions or states that occur in the past, present, or future.
The tenses are not tied to the aspect of the verb, which is determined by aspect words.

The vowels in Arcadia have a special role in the formation of the tenses.

## Timeless tenses

Arcadia has two timeless tenses, the veritas tense, and the status.

### Veritas

The veritas tense is used to describe a timeless truth, a scientific fact, or an unchanging state, anything that is true regardless of past, present, or future.
In the indicative mood, it's formed using the "&{-uv}" suffix.

### Status

The status tense is used to describe something regardless of past, present, or future, but it's not necessarily lasting forever.
In the indicative mood, it's formed using the "&{-iv}" suffix.

### Examples

1.  _&{glakuve akvatis in 0°C.}_

    - **translation:** Water freezes at 0°C.
    - **explanation:** As a scientific truth, it cannot change.

1.  _&{edrivo postcenaton post cenate.}_

    - **translation:** I always eat dessert after dinner.
    - **translation:** This is something that can change.

## Timed tenses

There are 3 timed tenses, and their suffixes are:

- **Present:** "-av"
- **Past:** "-ev"
- **Future:** "-ov"
