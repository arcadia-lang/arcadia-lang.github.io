# ⏳ Arcadian Verb Tense System

Arcadia distinguishes between **five verb tenses**:

- **Two timeless tenses** (not bound by chronological time)
- **Three timed tenses** (anchored in past, present, or future)

Tense indicates the **temporal framework** of a verb’s action or state.
[Aspect][aspect], however, is handled separately via aspectual modifiers and is **not embedded** in the tense suffix.

Tense is marked by **vowel-based suffixes**, which are appended to the verb stem **after** the `-ir` infinitive ending is dropped, and **before** the person-number suffix.

---

## 🌌 Timeless Tenses

These forms describe states that transcend conventional chronology—either absolute truths or persistent habits.

### 📘 Veritas Tense `-uv`

Used for universal truths, scientific facts, and metaphysical constants.

> Example:
> `glakuve to hidreis in 0°C.` > _Water freezes at 0°C._

**Note**: While specific conditions may vary, the truth is understood under Arcadia’s standard assumptions.

---

### 📗 Status Tense `-ev`

Used for habitual, ongoing, or culturally stable states that are not strictly timeless.

> Example:
> `edruvo to postcenun post to dicene.` > _I always eat dessert after lunch._

**Note**: This reflects a habit, not a law.
Compare:

- `ya postcenun` = generic dessert
- `to postcenun` = habitual reference to the act

---

## 🕰️ Timed Tenses

Timed tenses are tied to a specific temporal frame.

| Tense   | Suffix | Meaning                         |
| ------- | ------ | ------------------------------- |
| Present | `-av`  | current action/state            |
| Past    | `-iv`  | completed or prior action       |
| Future  | `-ov`  | anticipated or projected action |

These suffixes are placed **after the stem** and **before** the person-number suffix.

> Example with verb _sir_ (“to be”):
> `save` → it is
> `sive` → it was
> `sove` → it will be

---

## 🧠 Mnemonic Guide

Each tense vowel reflects its metaphysical quality:

- **_a_ → Present**: ephemeral, unfolding
- **_i_ → Past**: concluded, immutable
- **_o_ → Future**: uncertain, forming
- **_u_ → Veritas**: abstract, eternal
- **_e_ → Status**: stable, routine

This vowel system reinforces Arcadia’s aesthetic of harmony, temporality, and philosophical transparency.
