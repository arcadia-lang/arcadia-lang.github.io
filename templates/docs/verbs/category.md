# Verb Classification

Arcadia features a rich verb classification system, where each verb type follows specific syntactic rules.

- The syntax of each sentence stems from the verb structure and determines word order.
- The default word order in Arcadia is VSO (Verb-Subject-Object).

---

## Linking Verbs

Linking verbs do not express an action, but instead describe a state or characteristic (e.g., "I am a doctor", "The roses smell nice").

- Syntax: Verb - Subject - Attribute, where the attribute is in the predicative case.

Example:

- _perolfaves tas rososis amaresi._ → "The roses smell sweet."

One important linking verb use is introducing oneself:

- _seve de me namis jon snow._ → "I'm Jon Snow."

---

## Impersonal Verbs

Impersonal verbs describe general states of the world, locations, or time periods (e.g., _"It snows"_).

- These verbs lack a subject and do not take personal suffixes.

Example:

- _nivav._ → "It is snowing."

---

## Intransitive Verbs

Intransitive verbs describe actions without a recipient (e.g., _"The birds are singing"_) or states (e.g., _"The cat is sleeping"_).

- Syntax: Verb - Subject, where the subject is in the nominative case.

Example:

- _cantaves yes avisis._ → "Birds are singing."

---

## Transitive Verbs

Transitive verbs describe actions that have a recipient (e.g., _"I'm drinking water"_).

- Syntax: Verb - Subject - Object, where:
    - The subject is in the nominative case.
    - The object is in the accusative case.
- The subject can be dropped, especially in the first, second, and fourth person.

Example:

- _potavo y' akvon._ → "I'm drinking water."

---

## Ditransitive Verbs

Ditransitive verbs describe actions with two recipients (e.g., _"I'm giving the book to you"_).

- Syntax: Verb - Subject - Direct Object - Indirect Object, where:
    - The subject is in the nominative case.
    - The direct object is in the accusative case.
    - The indirect object is in the dative case.
- The subject can be dropped, especially in the first, second, and fourth person.

Examples:

- _donavo la libron veim._ → "I am giving the book to you."
- _donavo la libron la filim._ → "I am giving the book to the child."

---

## Object Promotion

Arcadia allows both direct and indirect objects to be promoted to subject, which occurs through verb derivation.

- Indirect Object (IO) promotion → "re-" prefix
- Direct Object (DO) promotion → "ob-" prefix

✔ After promotion:

- The promoted argument takes the nominative case.
- The demoted argument moves to the ablative case.

Examples:

- _potavo y' akvon._ → "I'm drinking water."
- _repotave y' akvis mea._ → "Water is being drunk by me."
- _donavo ta libron veim._ → "I am giving the book to you."
- _redonave ta libris mea veim._ → "The book is being given from me to you."
- _obdonavis ta libron mea._ → "You are receiving a book from me."

---

## Reflexivity and Reciprocity

Arcadia uses prefixes to mark reflexive and reciprocal actions.

- Reflexive: "aut-" prefix → Indicates self-directed action.
- Reciprocal: "con-" prefix → Indicates mutual action between two subjects.

Examples:

- _autamores adamis i marias._ → "Adam and Maria love themselves."
- _conamores adamis i marias._ → "Adam and Maria love each other."
