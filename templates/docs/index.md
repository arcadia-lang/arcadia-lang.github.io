# <x-trans>Arcadia</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia is a **highly expressive conlang** built on **consistency and clarity**—without exceptions.</x-trans>

<x-trans>It follows a **VSO (Verb-Subject-Object) syntax** and draws inspiration from **Romance languages** for vocabulary, orthography, and pronunciation.</x-trans>
<x-trans>The writing system ensures a **1:1 correspondence** between spoken and written forms, making it **ideal for machine parsing** while maintaining rich expressiveness.</x-trans>

<x-trans>Though this expressiveness introduces a **steeper learning curve**, the **absence of exceptions** provides a logical, predictable structure.</x-trans>

## <x-trans>Overview of Arcadia</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia is designed to be **a highly regular language**, eliminating irregularities and exceptions.</x-trans>
<x-trans>While **grammar and syntax** innovate beyond traditional linguistic patterns, phonetics, writing, and vocabulary remain **intentionally familiar**, as these aspects are considered **less central to linguistic structure**.</x-trans>

<x-trans>The language prioritizes **expressive precision**, always favoring **clarity of meaning over simplicity**—ensuring depth without unnecessary complexity.</x-trans>

### <x-trans>Literal Information Flow</x-trans>

<x-trans>A core principle in Arcadia is the **explicit communication of information**, avoiding assumptions.</x-trans>
<x-trans>For example:</x-trans>

- <x-trans>In English, "The door was black" suggests the color has changed.</x-trans>
- <x-trans>In Arcadia, _&{sive la portas neri}_ simply states **the door was black**, without implying any present change.</x-trans>

<x-trans>This **literal structure** ensures that **meaning is conveyed with precision** rather than inferred.</x-trans>

<x-trans>While Arcadia's **orthography and vocabulary** reflect Romance languages, its **grammar and syntax** incorporate influences from **natural languages and conlangs**, driving innovation.</x-trans>
