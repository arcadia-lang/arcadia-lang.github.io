### _mal_

One of the most common prefixes in Arcadia is _mal_, which means "bad".
It can be used to change the meaning of a noun to its opposite, in cases that the pair of antonyms can be associated with a positive/negative connotation.
For example, _veriti_ (truth) can be changed to _malveriti_ (falsehood), but _luxo_ (light) cannot be changed to _malluxo_ (darkness), as the light/dark distinction is not based on a positive/negative connotation.

Nouns that can use the _mal_ prefix include:

- veriti: truth

### _sin_

One other way to create antonyms is to use the _sin_ prefix.
This is used on words that the antonym is based on the absence, or less quantity of the original meaning.
For example, _luxo_ (light) can be changed to _sinluxo_ (darkness), as darkness is the absence of light.

Nouns that can use the _sin_ prefix include:

- luxo: light
- caloro: heat

### _opo(n)_

One other way to create antonyms is to use the _opo(n)_ prefix.
This is a generic "opposite of" prefix, and it's used in all other cases.
If the noun starts with a vowel or voiceless consonant, the _n_ is dropped.

## Countries, Languages, and People

Arcadia has a way to generate names for languages and people based on the country.
The countries are always solid nouns.
The language is added by adding the suffix _-a_ to the country name, and the person from the country is added by adding the suffix _-ane_, and the associated adjective by adding _-ani_.
Not all countries have a corresponding language, for example, _helveti_ (Switzerland) does not have a corresponding language, as the country is multilingual.
Also, some languages do not have a corresponding country, for example, _esperantia_ (Esperanto) is a language that does not have a corresponding country.

### Examples

- _itali_ (Italy) ->

  - _italia_ (Italian language, noun)
  - _italiane_ (Italian person, noun)
  - _italiani_ (Italian, adjective)

- _greci_ (Greece) ->
  - _grecia_ (Greek language, noun)
  - _greciane_ (Greek person, noun)
  - _greciani_ (Greek, adjective)

## Abstraction and Concreteness

Arcadia has a way to generate abstract nouns from concrete nouns, and vice versa.
Let's explore how this works.

### Abstraction

In Arcadia, abstract nouns are often formed by adding the suffix _-nthu_ to a noun.
For example, the noun _libri_ (book) can be turned into the abstract noun _librinthu_ (the bookness). This is not the same as using the article _to_ (the abstract article), which is used to refer to abstract concepts in a more general sense.
