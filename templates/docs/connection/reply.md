# <x-trans>Replies</x-trans>

## **<x-trans>Reply Words</x-trans>**

<x-trans>Arcadia has **three primary reply words**, each serving a distinct conversational function:</x-trans>

- **si** → **"<x-trans>Yes</x-trans>"** (<x-trans>affirmative response</x-trans>).
- **ni** → **"<x-trans>No</x-trans>"** (<x-trans>negative response</x-trans>).
- **ack** → **<x-trans>Neutral acknowledgment</x-trans>**—<x-trans>used to show that the listener is paying attention or understands **without confirming or denying** the statement.</x-trans>

- [x] **<x-trans>Usage of "ack"</x-trans>** → <x-trans>Commonly used in **dialogue** when one wants to **indicate understanding** but avoid committing to a definitive stance.</x-trans>

## **<x-trans>Generic Reply Words</x-trans>**

<x-trans>Similar to English words like **"nowhere," "somewhere," "anywhere," and "everywhere,"** Arcadia has **structured generic reply words** that align with **interrogatives**.</x-trans>

### **<x-trans>Interrogative-Based Reply Forms</x-trans>**

<x-trans>Each interrogative combines with **prefixes** to generate **negative, generic, specific, and collective** responses:</x-trans>

| **<x-trans>Interrogative</x-trans>** | **<x-trans>Negative</x-trans>**             | **<x-trans>Unknown (generic)</x-trans>**     | **<x-trans>Unknown (specific)</x-trans>**      | **<x-trans>Collective</x-trans>**                |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **ce** _(who/what)_                  | **ane** (<x-trans>no one/nothing</x-trans>) | **aye** (<x-trans>anyone/anything</x-trans>) | **eye** (<x-trans>someone/something</x-trans>) | **are** (<x-trans>everyone/everything</x-trans>) |
| **cuál** _(which)_                   | **anuál** (<x-trans>none</x-trans>)         | **ayuál** (<x-trans>any</x-trans>)           | **eyuál** (<x-trans>some</x-trans>)            | **aruál** (<x-trans>all</x-trans>)               |
| **cove** _(where)_                   | **anove** (<x-trans>nowhere</x-trans>)      | **ayove** (<x-trans>anywhere</x-trans>)      | **eyove** (<x-trans>somewhere</x-trans>)       | **arove** (<x-trans>everywhere</x-trans>)        |
| **cuando** _(when)_                  | **anuando** (<x-trans>never</x-trans>)      | **ayuando** (<x-trans>anytime</x-trans>)     | **eyuando** (<x-trans>sometime</x-trans>)      | **aruando** (<x-trans>always</x-trans>)          |
| **cuót** _(how much/how many)_       | **anuót** (<x-trans>none</x-trans>)         | **ayuót** (<x-trans>any amount</x-trans>)    | **eyuót** (<x-trans>some amount</x-trans>)     | **aruót** (<x-trans>all</x-trans>)               |
| **cume** _(how)_                     | **anume** (<x-trans>no way</x-trans>)       | **ayume** (<x-trans>any way</x-trans>)       | **eyume** (<x-trans>some way</x-trans>)        | **arume** (<x-trans>every way</x-trans>)         |

- [x] **<x-trans>Prefix System</x-trans>:**

    - **an-** → <x-trans>Negative form (absence or negation).</x-trans>
    - **ay-** → <x-trans>Unknown generic (any).</x-trans>
    - **ey-** → <x-trans>Unknown specific (some).</x-trans>
    - **ar-** → <x-trans>Collective reference (all).</x-trans>
