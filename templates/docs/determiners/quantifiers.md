# Quantifiers

Quantifiers are **determiners** that indicate the **quantity of a noun**.

- [x] **Nullar and collective numbers** → Naturally make **"all" and "none" redundant**, simplifying expressions.
- [x] **Usage Restrictions** → **Quantifiers cannot be used with uncountable nouns**, since the **number already conveys quantity**.

### **Core Quantifiers**

Arcadia defines the following **quantifiers**:

- **_poco_** → **"A few"** (small but distinct amount).
- **_aliame_** → **"Some"** (can refer to one or multiple).
- **_oliame_** → **"Some"** (cannot refer to one, only plural).
- **_multe_** → **"Many"** (large quantity).
- **_cisca_** → **"Each"** (individual emphasis).

### **Usage Notes**

- [x] **"aliame"** → Even though it **can refer to one**, it always **takes a plural noun form**.
- [x] **"cisca"** →
  - Used with **singular nouns** to highlight **individual entities**.
  - Used with **collective nouns** to emphasize **both individuals and the group**.

---

## **Examples**

- [x] **Collective Focus (`&{puereris}` in collective form)**
  - _edravur puereris e pomon_ → **"Every child is eating an apple."**
  - **Explanation:** The **collective noun** and verb highlight **the group as a whole**.

- [x] **Singular Emphasis (`cisca` with singular noun)**
  - _edrave cisca pueris e pomon_ → **"Each child is eating an apple."**
  - **Explanation:** The verb and noun remain **singular**, focusing on **individual children**.

- [x] **Hybrid Emphasis (`cisca` with collective noun)**
  - _edravur cisca puereris e pomon_ → **"Each and every child is eating an apple."**
  - **Explanation:** The **collective noun + cisca** highlights **both individuals and the group**.
