# <x-trans>Demonstratives</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia has three **bound deixis elements**, that are **never used alone**.</x-trans>
<x-trans>Instead, they **attach to interrogatives** to add **spatial or temporal deixis**, specifying proximity or distance.</x-trans>

<x-trans>The **three levels of deixis** are:</x-trans>

- **_est:_** <x-trans>Close (near the speaker).</x-trans>
- **_ess:_** <x-trans>Far (visible but distant).</x-trans>
- **_tis:_** <x-trans>Very far or invisible (out of sight, abstract, or distant in time).</x-trans>

<x-trans>Since **&{est}, &{ess}, and &{tis}** are dependent modifiers, they **cannot appear independently** in speech or writing.</x-trans>
<x-trans>They always require an **interrogative base** to function correctly.</x-trans>

### **<x-trans>Interrogative Demonstratives</x-trans>**

<x-trans>Deixis elements are combined with **interrogatives** to form precise demonstratives:</x-trans>

- **_ce:_** (<x-trans>who/what</x-trans>)

    - **<x-trans>Close</x-trans>:** _este_ → "<x-trans>This</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Far</x-trans>:** _esse_ → "<x-trans>That</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Very far/invisible</x-trans>:** _tise_ → "<x-trans>The one that's out of my sight</x-trans>"

- **_cuál:_** (<x-trans>which</x-trans>)

    - **<x-trans>Close</x-trans>:** _estuál_ → "<x-trans>This one</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Far</x-trans>:** _essuál_ → "<x-trans>That one</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Very far/invisible</x-trans>:** _tisuál_ → "<x-trans>The one that's out of my sight</x-trans>"

- **_cove:_** (<x-trans>where</x-trans>)

    - **<x-trans>Close</x-trans>:** _estove_ → "<x-trans>Here</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Far</x-trans>:** _essove_ → "<x-trans>There</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Very far/invisible</x-trans>:** _tisove_ → "<x-trans>The place that's out of sight</x-trans>"

- **_cuando:_** (<x-trans>when</x-trans>)

    - **<x-trans>Close</x-trans>:** _estuando_ → "<x-trans>Now</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Far</x-trans>:** _essuando_ → "<x-trans>The other time</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Very far/invisible</x-trans>:** _tisuando_ → "<x-trans>In a distant time</x-trans>"

- **_cuót:_** (<x-trans>how much/how many</x-trans>)

    - **<x-trans>Close</x-trans>:** _estuót_ → "<x-trans>This much</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Far</x-trans>:** _essuót_ → "<x-trans>That much</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Very far/invisible</x-trans>:** _tisuót_ → "<x-trans>That much</x-trans>"

- **_cume:_** (<x-trans>how</x-trans>)

    - **<x-trans>Close</x-trans>:** _estume_ → "<x-trans>This way</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Far</x-trans>:** _essume_ → "<x-trans>That way</x-trans>"
    - **<x-trans>Very far/invisible</x-trans>:** _tisume_ → "<x-trans>That way</x-trans>"

!!! note "<x-trans>Deictic Expressions</x-trans>"

    <x-trans>_"-&{ove}"_, _"-&{uando}"_, and _"-&{ume}"_ are **not true demonstratives**, but are included here due to their close relationship with **deictic expressions**.</x-trans>

---

## **<x-trans>Agreement Rules</x-trans>**

- [x] <x-trans>When combined with a noun, **the demonstrative must agree in number**.</x-trans>
- [x] <x-trans>Demonstratives **do not take case markings** when used with a noun (to avoid redundancy).</x-trans>
- [x] <x-trans>When **used independently**, case marking **is mandatory**.</x-trans>

---

## **<x-trans>"&{ess}" vs. "&{tis}" in Temporal Contexts</x-trans>**

- [x] **"essando"** → <x-trans>Used when referring to **a time within a lifetime**.</x-trans>
- [x] **"tisando"** → <x-trans>Used when referring to **a more distant time or an abstract concept**.</x-trans>

---

## **<x-trans>Examples</x-trans>**

- [x] **<x-trans>Demonstrative with a noun</x-trans>** → <x-trans>No case marking required:</x-trans>

    - _este pomos_ → **"<x-trans>This apple</x-trans>"**
    - _estes pomosis_ → **"<x-trans>These apples</x-trans>"**
    - _estuál pomos_ → **"<x-trans>This one apple</x-trans>"** (<x-trans>choosing among apples</x-trans>)
    - _estót aguas_ → **"<x-trans>This much water</x-trans>"**

- [x] **<x-trans>Demonstrative as a standalone pronoun</x-trans>** → <x-trans>Requires case marking:</x-trans>

    - _volavo esseon._ → **"<x-trans>I want that.</x-trans>"** (<x-trans>Case marking required</x-trans>)
    - _volavo tiseon._ → **"<x-trans>I want the one that's out of sight.</x-trans>"**
