# Articles

Arcadia has **five articles**, each serving a distinct grammatical function:

- **Definite (`la`)** → _la leon_ → **"The lion"** (a specific, known lion).
- **Definite-Anaphoric (`le`)** → _le leon_ → **"The lion (we talked about earlier)"** (referencing a previously mentioned lion).
- **Indefinite-Weak (`ya`)** → _ya leon_ → **"Any lion"** (a general reference to any lion).
- **Indefinite-Strong (`ye`)** → _ye leon_ → **"A lion (one specific but unknown)"** (introducing a singular but particular lion).
- **Collective (`ta`)** → _ta leon_ → **"A pride of lions"** (encapsulating collective meaning).

### **Usage Rules**

✔ **Definite article (`&{la}/&{le}`)** can modify **indefinite determiners**, turning them **definite**:

- _tre homosis_ → **"three people"**
- _las tre homosis_ → **"three specific people"**

✔ **Indefinite article (`&{ya}/&{ye}`)** can transform proper nouns into **common nouns**:

- _ye adamis_ → **"a person named Adam"**

---

## **Examples**

1. _convenivo adamon._

    - **Translation:** "I met Adam."
    - **Explanation:** Refers to a **specific person named Adam** that the speaker knows.

1. _convenivo le adamon._

    - **Translation:** "I met Adam."
    - **Explanation:** Refers to a **specific person named Adam** that was mentioned before.

1. _convenivo ye adamon._

    - **Translation:** "I met a guy named Adam."
    - **Explanation:** Refers to **someone named Adam**, without assuming familiarity.

### **Pronunciation Adjustments**

✔ If a singular article **precedes a noun beginning with the same vowel**, it **contracts**, replacing the vowel with an apostrophe:

- **la** → **l'** (_la amigos_ → _l' amicus_)
- **le** → **l'** (_le energias_ → _l' energias_)
- **ya** → **y'** (_ya avis_ → _y' avis_)
- **ye** → **y'** (_ye enginas_ → _y' enginas_)
- **ta** → **t'** (_ta antecenus_ → _t' antecenus_)

This ensures **smooth pronunciation** while maintaining clarity in speech.
