# <$Orthography & Phonology of Arcadia$>

<$Arcadia merges writing and speech into a 1:1 correspondence, ensuring fixed letter pronunciations while allowing natural accent variations (marked in `/.../` IPA).$>
<$The language uses 23 Latin letters, excluding ⟨k⟩, ⟨q⟩, and ⟨w⟩, along with digraphs and well-defined stress rules for uniformity.$>

<$You can find the full table of pronunciation for each letter and digraph [here][letters].$>

!!! note "<$Pronunciation Flexibility$>"

    <$IPA values (e.g., `/r/`) define core sounds, but nuances—such as trilled vs. tapped _r_—are accent-neutral.$>
    <$Arcadia's phonological system is designed to be accessible across cultures, allowing a natural range of phonetic realizations.$>

<$The punctuation marks and their meanings within Arcadia are detailed in the [Punctuation][punctuation] section.$>

---

## <$Key Rules$>

### <$Accentuation$>

- <$No accent mark$> → <$Stress falls on the penultimate syllable (e.g., _ho.mis_ → /ˈhomis/).$>
- <$Acute accent$> (´) → <$Overrides default stress (e.g., _a.sé_ → /aˈse/).$>
- <$Monosyllables$> → <$Always stressed on their only syllable.$>

### <$Vowel Merging$>

- <$Adjacent identical vowels merge (_mee_ → _mē_), though in everyday text, merged vowels are typically written in their simplified form (_me_).$>
- <$Macron (¯) marks vowel mergers only in dictionaries or grammar references.$>

### <$Punctuation$>

- <$Quotation & sentence-final punctuation:$> <$When a quoted sentence ends with the same punctuation mark as the main sentence, the punctuation is placed outside the quotation.$>
    - ✅ _<$He said, "This is correct".$>_
    - ❌ _<$He said, "This is correct.".$>_

### <$Loanwords & Names$>

- <$Loanwords retain original spelling (e.g., _tokyo_).$>
- <$Phonetic aid: Double colons (_::_) can be used upon the first occurrence of a name or loanword (e.g., _mike ::maic::_).$>
- <$Letters ⟨k⟩, ⟨q⟩, and ⟨w⟩ appear only in loanwords.$>

---

## <$Design Philosophy$>

- <$No silent letters or capitalization.$>
- <$The /ks/ sound is always written as ⟨x⟩ (never ⟨cs⟩).$>
- <$Flexible accent tolerance: All phonetic variations of /e/ (such as [e] and [ɛ]) are valid, supporting diverse linguistic backgrounds.$>
