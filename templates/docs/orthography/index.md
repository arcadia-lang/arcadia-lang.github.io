# <x-trans>Orthography of Arcadia</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia uses **23 letters** from the Latin alphabet, **excluding &{k}, &{q}, and &{w}**.</x-trans>
<x-trans>Each letter has a **fixed pronunciation**, ensuring a **consistent spelling-to-sound correspondence**.</x-trans>

## <x-trans>Digraphs</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia also uses the following **digraphs**:</x-trans>

| <x-trans>Digraph</x-trans> | <x-trans>IPA</x-trans> | <x-trans>English Approximation</x-trans> |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| ch                         | /t͜s/                   | <x-trans>**ch**air</x-trans>             |
| dh                         | /ð/                    | <x-trans>**th**ere</x-trans>             |
| th                         | /θ/                    | <x-trans>**th**anks</x-trans>            |

!!! tip "<x-trans>Rule</x-trans>"

    <x-trans>The phoneme **/&{ks}/** is always spelled **"&{x}"**, never as "&{cs}."</x-trans>

!!! warning "<x-trans>Pronunciation</x-trans>"

    <x-trans>Each letter is pronounced according to its **IPA value** and may differ slightly from English pronunciation.</x-trans>
    <x-trans>The **English equivalent** provides only the closest approximation (based on Received Pronunciation).</x-trans>

## <x-trans>Accentuation</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia uses the **acute accent (&{´})** to mark stress:</x-trans>

- <x-trans>Words **without an acute**</x-trans> → <x-trans>Default stress on the **penultimate syllable** (or the **only syllable** if the word is monosyllabic).</x-trans>
- <x-trans>Words **with an acute**</x-trans> → <x-trans>Stress falls on the **marked syllable**.</x-trans>

<x-trans>Examples:</x-trans>

- **homis** (_<x-trans>human, person</x-trans>_) → <x-trans>stress on "o"</x-trans> (**ho**mis).
- **ni** (_<x-trans>no</x-trans>_) → <x-trans>stress on "i"</x-trans> (**ni**).
- **asé** (_<x-trans>third-person pronoun</x-trans>_) → <x-trans>stress on "é"</x-trans> (as**é**).

## <x-trans>Vowel Merging</x-trans> & <x-trans>Macron Notation</x-trans>

<x-trans>When **compounding words** or **adding affixes**, **identical adjacent vowels** are **never allowed**—they always **merge into a single instance**.</x-trans>

!!! tip "<x-trans>Rule</x-trans>"

    <x-trans>In **dictionary entries, grammar books, and syntax guides**, vowel mergers can be marked with a **macron** over the vowel (e.g., _mē_ instead of _me_).</x-trans>
    <x-trans>However, **macrons are not used in normal writing**—words are spelled without them in everyday text.</x-trans>

## <x-trans>Punctuation</x-trans> & <x-trans>Formatting</x-trans>

<x-trans>Arcadia has **no capitalization** and **no silent letters**.</x-trans>
<x-trans>It uses the following **punctuation marks**:</x-trans>

| <x-trans>Mark</x-trans> | <x-trans>Name</x-trans>             | <x-trans>Usage</x-trans>                                                                      |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| .                       | <x-trans>Period</x-trans>           | <x-trans>Ends a declarative sentence.</x-trans>                                               |
| ;                       | <x-trans>Semicolon</x-trans>        | <x-trans>Connects related independent clauses or separates items in a complex list.</x-trans> |
| ,                       | <x-trans>Comma</x-trans>            | <x-trans>Separates elements in a list or clauses within a sentence.</x-trans>                 |
| :                       | <x-trans>Colon</x-trans>            | <x-trans>Introduces a list, quotation, or explanation.</x-trans>                              |
| !                       | <x-trans>Exclamation Mark</x-trans> | <x-trans>Expresses strong emotion or a command.</x-trans>                                     |
| ?                       | <x-trans>Question Mark</x-trans>    | <x-trans>Marks a question.</x-trans>                                                          |
| ""                      | <x-trans>Quotation Marks</x-trans>  | <x-trans>Encloses direct speech, quotations, or titles.</x-trans>                             |

### **<x-trans>Hyphens</x-trans> & <x-trans>Apostrophes</x-trans>**

- **<x-trans>Hyphens</x-trans> (-)** → <x-trans>Used for compound words forming a single lexeme.</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Apostrophes</x-trans> (’)** → <x-trans>Used for contractions.</x-trans>
- **<x-trans>Single Quotes</x-trans> (')** → <x-trans>Can substitute apostrophes for easier typing.</x-trans>

!!! note "<x-trans>Punctuation</x-trans> & <x-trans>Quotes</x-trans>"

    Punctuation should be placed **outside** quotation marks **unless** both the quoted sentence and the main sentence **end simultaneously**.

## <x-trans>Proper Nouns</x-trans> & <x-trans>Loanwords</x-trans>

- <x-trans>Proper names **retain their original spelling** (if in Latin alphabet).</x-trans>
- <x-trans>Names from other scripts **use standard Latin transliteration**, or a phonetic adaptation.</x-trans>
- <x-trans>Even with original spelling, names are pronounced **as close as possible** to the source language.</x-trans>
- <x-trans>As a **reader's aid**, a name can be **transliterated inside double colons** the first time it appears:</x-trans>
    - <x-trans>Example:</x-trans> "`mike::maic::`"
- <x-trans>Loanwords may contain the letters &{k}, &{q}, or &{w}, even though they do not appear in native Arcadian words.</x-trans>
