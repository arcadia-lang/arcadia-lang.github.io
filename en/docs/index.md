# Arcadia

Arcadia is a **highly expressive conlang** built on **consistency and clarity**—without exceptions.

It follows a **VSO (Verb-Subject-Object) syntax** and draws inspiration from **Romance languages** for vocabulary, orthography, and pronunciation.
The writing system ensures a **1:1 correspondence** between spoken and written forms, making it **ideal for machine parsing** while maintaining rich expressiveness.

Though this expressiveness introduces a **steeper learning curve**, the **absence of exceptions** provides a logical, predictable structure.

## Overview of Arcadia

Arcadia is designed to be **a highly regular language**, eliminating irregularities and exceptions.
While **grammar and syntax** innovate beyond traditional linguistic patterns, phonetics, writing, and vocabulary remain **intentionally familiar**, as these aspects are considered **less central to linguistic structure**.

The language prioritizes **expressive precision**, always favoring **clarity of meaning over simplicity**—ensuring depth without unnecessary complexity.

### Literal Information Flow

A core principle in Arcadia is the **explicit communication of information**, avoiding assumptions.
For example:

- In English, "The door was black" suggests the color has changed.
- In Arcadia, _sive la portas neri_ simply states **the door was black**, without implying any present change.

This **literal structure** ensures that **meaning is conveyed with precision** rather than inferred.

While Arcadia's **orthography and vocabulary** reflect Romance languages, its **grammar and syntax** incorporate influences from **natural languages and conlangs**, driving innovation.

## Key Features of Arcadia

Arcadia introduces several **unique** or **rare linguistic features**, including:

- **Four-person system**
- **Timeless tenses**
- **Factual conditionals in the past**
