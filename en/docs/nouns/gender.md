# Gender

Arcadia has **five grammatical genders**, which serve as **structural classifications** rather than semantic distinctions.
Each gender is named after a prototype noun:

- **terre** (Earth)
- **hidri** (Water)
- **fuega** (Fire)
- **aero** (Air)
- **etheru** (Aether)

All nouns in Arcadia **end in a vowel**, and their gender is determined by the **final vowel**.

However, Arcadia itself is **not a gendered language**—it does not inherently mark nouns for male or female identity.
While gender-specific prefixes exist, they are optional:

- **"pa-"** (male)
- **"ma-"** (female)

When gender **is not relevant**, the noun remains unmarked.

!!! note "Using Non-Grammatical Gender"

    By default, non-grammatical gender **should not** be marked unless explicitly relevant.
    For example, "datere" (parent) is used unless specifying "padatere" (father) or "madatere" (mother).

## Examples

- **Lion** → _leona_
    - **Male**: _paleona_
    - **Female**: _maleona_
