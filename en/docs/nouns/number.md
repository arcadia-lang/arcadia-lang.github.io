# Number

Numbers are formed by adding a **suffix** to the root word.
The semantic meaning of the suffix **varies** depending on whether the noun is **countable or uncountable**.

- [x] If the noun **ends in a consonant**, an **"e" is inserted** between the base and the suffix.

---

## **Countable Nouns**

Countable nouns are those that can be **quantified as discrete units**—such as apples, kilograms, and humans.
Using **"dia"** (_day_), here are the suffixes:

- **Singular:** No suffix—used for exactly one item (**dia** → a day).
- **Fractional (`-d`)** → Represents a quantity **less than one** (**diad** → part of a day).
- **Plural (`-s`)** → Used for **more than one item**, even if not a full integer (**dias** → days).
- **Nullar (`-n`)** → Expresses **absence** of the noun (**dian** → no days).
- **Collective (`-r`)** → Represents the **totality** of the noun (**diar** → all days).

✔ **Negative quantities** take the **same suffix** as their positive counterpart.

---

## **Uncountable Nouns**

Uncountable nouns do **not** have discrete units—such as water, money, and love.
Using **"amore"** (_love_), the suffixes and their meanings are:

- **Singular:** No suffix—used when quantity is unspecified (**amore** → love).
- **Fractional (`-d`)** → Represents a **small** quantity (**amored** → a little bit of love).
- **Plural (`-s`)** → Expresses a **large** quantity (**amores** → an abundance of love).
- **Nullar (`-n`)** → Indicates **absence** (**amoren** → no love).
- **Collective (`-r`)** → Represents the **totality of the concept** (**amorer** → all love in existence).
