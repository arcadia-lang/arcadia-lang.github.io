# Nouns

Nouns form the foundation of any language.
In Arcadia, they are classified based on their characteristics and usage.

One way to categorize nouns is by specificity:

- **Proper nouns** refer to a unique entity (_Arcadia, Maria_).
- **Common nouns** describe a general concept (_tree, apple_).

Another categorization is by [gender][gender], though Arcadia does **not** distinguish between masculine, feminine, or neuter nouns.
Instead, it uses **five grammatical genders**, which serve only as structural classifications without semantic meaning.
A **gender prefix** may be used for clarity, but is generally optional.

Arcadia also distinguishes nouns by [grammatical number][number].
The meaning of each number depends on whether the noun is **countable** or **uncountable**.

Finally, nouns follow seven [cases][case], which define their role within a sentence.
